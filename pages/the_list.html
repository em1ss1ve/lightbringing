I provide you with this bit of reasoning; everything in the material world is subject to decay and destruction, and the only thing that can truly be brought with you are the knowledge and experience which you have gained, and other purviews of the mind. In addition, it it is the internal state of the individual which is most responsible for their life, (when their most basic needs are met) so it is of paramount importance to prioritize the creation of an ultimate state of being which is only mediated by the whims of others insofar as they reside in compassion and benefit to others.

I envision a world where people are satisfied with their inner worlds (and the process of sharing them with others) to a degree where they need basically nothing exterior in their lives and can still find them incredibly fulfilling. I will provide an example of this. There is a factory worker, who lives with two other people in a house which is big enough to where they all have the option to spread out as much as they need to, but given that they have almost nothing to their names (save for a method of storing and accessing the worlds which they have created and the artifacts which they have created for those worlds) they crave for no additional space. This person has a few plants to their name, and their room is finely crafted yet minimal. They possess enough clothes for exactly eight days, and these clothes are both extremely durable and comfortable. Given the lack of objects, the space is easy to clean and maintain, and its spartan nature is comforting to its inhabitant, who finds that the lack of distractions makes it easier for them to feel at ease in the space. In their free time, they create; they spend the time they have building out intricate worlds and societies and architecture and experiences, and they share them with their roommates (who work on similar projects in their free time). Together they share a project, one which they have all contributed to and which they can all interact with. They have created characters which reside in this world, and they have taken the time to thoroughly customise and individualise these characters, giving them distinct and highly developed personalities. They have also contributed other characters to inhabit this world, and when it comes time for one of their characters around whom the story which they are collectively telling is centered decides to interact with one of these other 'world' characters (there are then personal characters and world characters), the person who is responsible for creating this character takes the time to embody the personality of this person and engages with the other individuals in this world. Given the fact that there is a standardised (if flexible) set of rules by which individuals can take actions in other's worlds, it becomes very easy to engage with someone's world for the first time, as they are then responsible for determining how your engagement effects the world, and you are responsible for choosing the actions that you wish to take in that world. To return to the example, this factory worker has been spending the last couple days finely tuning the imagined experience of a town, which, as a result of their choice to consider the implications that a lack of gravity would have on the social and architectural expression of culture over time and the plethora of other alterations to their way of life, they go to their co-habitants with an explanation of the basic premise for this world, and they spend some time creating individuals who could believably be understood to reside within this culture. They spend some time exploring and creating this town and culture together, and they are then all left with this mental impression of this town which they can carry with them for the rest of their lives, which will offer them something to cconsider when they have a lull in their responsibilities. The most wonderful part of this is that the next day while the factory worker is at their job, because this is such a common practice that it has become second nature, this person can share in similar worlds with their co-workers, and as such, they are willing and excitied to go to work and to do their work (regardless of what it is) because it offers the opportunity to do something productive while simultaneously simple enough that it allows the mind time to wander and create. That day while they are at work, they notice that one of the machines that they are working with has an unintended feature of its design which makes it relatively uncomfortable to use, and they become interested enough with this problem that they decide to consider the problem, and because of this zero-gravity society which they have created for their own diversion, and into which they decided to pour a decent amount of research regarding the production of different materials and their traits under different environmental stressors), they realize that because of the way the material of the handle is a made from is interacting with the temperature accumulation in the main body of the tool, the differntial between the rate of expansion of the materials is causing a very gradual loosening of the binding between the handle and the barrel, and that this is what is causing the vibration which has begun to cause the tool to become noticably less comfortable to use over time. While they are not specialized in the field of tool design, through their own research they were able to gain the necessary information to diagnose the problem which faced them, and they were then able to pass this information on to the individual who is responsible for the provision of the tools, as well as a description of the specifics of the problem present within the object. The fact that they have generalized their studies in this way as a result of their interest in creating a particularly realistic world (not that this is a tendency which every individual prioritizes in the creation of their worlds) led to a solution to a problem which they would otherwise have not been able to solve were they focussed only on doing the bare minimum, and their transmission of this information to the people who are responsible for creating this tool resulted in a massive reduction to the amount of waste which would have been generated by the continued production of long-wear defective units, leading to an improvement in the quality of life for not just the production team responsible for creating the tool, but every individual who used those tools and in society, with the degree of their provided benefit increasing exponentially with every living being which lives during and after their time. It is in this way that every act, every choice, every single object brought into this world becomes something which will effect countless others, whether they are personally known or not. Every choice we make in every moment is, as a result of the chain of cause and effect, universe altering (though obviously to more or less subtle degrees). The choice to live in such a way which simultaneously maximises your potential as an individual while also limiting the ways which you contribute negatively is a choice which effects not only everyone you know, but also yourself, in that the choices yyou make alter the world, and you live in and rely on the world, because you are and are of the world. This is simultaneously the greatest gift and the greatest curse; In every moment you are taking actions which either benefit or harm the world, and this endows every moment with such a massive degree of power and sacredness which makes the profundity and meaning of your existence embedded into every moment and every experience, making every single individual responsible for the state of the world. Everything is choices, including a lack of action, including the re-inforcement of the status-quo through your engagement with it, leaving you personally responsible for the heavens and hells which you see in the lives of every entity surrounding you at every moment. Those choices which are the most pernicious are those which are only very slightly harmful but which are engaged with habitually or unconsciously, as these are the easiest to ignore. 3. Art Cult

3.1. Purpose

The guiding principle which motivates this section is one of exploring the causes of human motivation and orginizational tendencies, with the purpose of creating a working 'cult' for the production of art and various other imaginary creations, and to explore commonalities in art and religion. This will act as another means to ensure the creation of art, as it will enshrine creation as an act of servitude (as love) to the universe, to some divine force, for the purposes of 'salvation'. By salvation, what is intended is a re-ordering of the goals of the human species, that we will be shown a way towards sustainable and fulfilling living.

3.1.1. Justification

  1. Motivation (Meaning and Suffering)

    One of the greatest questions which human beings face is motivation. Motivating oneself to do things can be exceptionally difficult, but can become easier when the individual is in possession of a reason greater than themselves. We are facing a crisis of meaning in our society, and I see this as an outgrowth of a lack of percieved value and service to their actions and lives. Many people are able to operate at a basic level without meaning in their lives, but I have observed a lack of fulfillment, and a greater fracturing of the social structure, and I think that these are interconnected. Also at play is the understanding that the world is being consumed to death, which makes many with consciences feel that they are part of the problem, but they also don't know what they can do to help. These people wallow in consumption, suffering tremendously, and they have no meaning through which to fill the pit in their lives.

    Western culture has primarily developed around two threats; the threat of violence, and the threat of deprivation (though this is in many ways equally violent in its reality.) Should an individual choose not to conform to the rules which are set by the governing body, society has a (near) monopoly on the use of force. Those who choose not to conform to the social rules are frequently threatened with this same use of force, though not as overtly by the governing body as by its' constituents, of whom many are perfectly comfortable enforcing the status quo. This social violence can also manifest as deprivation (whether of opportunity, material, housing, connection, etc.), and can have results which are equally violent. Thus, the only way not to face this threat of violence is believed to be conformity to a certain socially established path of development and way of living, preventing the true social innovation which is required for a social evolution which is becoming increasingly important as the threat of global warming becomes pertinant. If there has ever been a time when non-conformity is important, it's now, and to a degree which is existential. Similarly, it seems obvious that our culture would be violent and depressed when the pillar upon which people are motivated to live and act is violence. Individuals feel like they have no power because they are unwilling to face the fact that the systems around them threaten their lives, as well as the fact that they have this same capacity for violence within them, and that they are contributors to this cruelty through their continued participation. People feel like they lack meaning and purpose in their lives because most are fundamentally compassionate in their core, and are stuck in a system which forces them to act in contradiction to this nature.

    The question of whether people are currently truly compassionate at their core is unnecessary, because if you believe that people are truly compassionate, then you will be truly compassionate, causing others to be compassionate.

    It seems that there are overlapping but disparate motivators for the totality of human activity: suffering, and meaning.

    Meaning is understood as both understandings of signs and symbols, and as a framework for living formed from human reason which drives specific action.

    Suffering is understood not to be the pain which humans experience but the attachment to not experiencing pain, as well as the attachment to experiencing pleasure. Suffering is the desire for something to be different than it is, whether it be pain, a lack of pleasure, an excess of something, etc. This is solved in two seemingly disparate but identical ways by Christianity and Buddhism. In Christianity, the problem of the individual suffering is solved by completely giving oneself over to the care of god with absolute trust. In Buddhism, this is solved by understanding and releasing the origin of the suffering. Once the origin of the suffering is identified as the craving for something not to be the case, the individual can surrender the desire, and the suffering falls away, and when completely internalized, this expands to an understanding which leads to a similarly complete surrender. In the state of surrender, one lives in a complete presence, and their compassionate fullness is revealed.

  2. In Defence of The Religious Artistic Mode of Being

    It has been observed that one of the best teachers of this state of surrender is the artistic process, so it is necessary to create a religious framework for the creation of art, as ritual can be exceptionally powerful at re-inforcing a state of flow in practitioners.

    The proof for the existence of a deity is not in the scope of this project, but it is necessary to explain why a deity has been included in this, as well as the framework through which it ought to be understood.

    The deity which this framework is included is one which both exists and does not; it will provide utility in life exactly proportional with belief. It exists as much as anything else which we interact with on a daily basis exists; it is a convenient collection of symbols through which to understand various states of experience, and postures for approaching the world, which is not representative of the true reality. Capitalism is a similar deity; it has certain signs, symbols, and understandings of the world which together constitute a way of living life, and functions as it does only because everyone believes it exists, yet it too is not actually representative of the true nature of reality.

    Imagine you have a sheet of paper. On this sheet of paper is written a promise that I will give you a shirt that I'm borrowing from you back, as I know how much you love this shirt, and that it also says that if I don't return that shirt, then you can get our shared neighbor to use the key he has to my house (as he sometimes comes over to water the plants) to either retrieve the shirt or take another similar shirt. Imagine as well that our shared neighbor has an incredibly strong belief in the power of promises, and you give him that sheet of paper (which now represents the imagined return of an object) in exchange for him to rake your leaves. This is how all of money works.

    Objects are abstracted into a representation of the energy (the effort applied over time), and this representation of the energy (which has only imagined meaning) can be exchanged for other objects, with the people with whom they are traded knowing that it means they have the imagined promise for future objects. This, like religion, only works because enough people believe in it.

    The Metaphysics are similar; they offer signs and symbols which provide a way of viewing life which is conducive to the creation of art, and are not meant to be understood as truly representing reality.

  3. Tidbits

    All art is a process of discovery, of the transformation of endless nothingness into something which has a degree of meaning or impression in the human mind greater than the original constituents required for its creation.

    We are facing a crisis in our species of our own making, and one might think that it would be strange for me to then suggest that more art to be created, but there is reason for this. People have for too long been relying on consuming other's creations, when these same things can feel more satisfying when engaged with in a less substantial realm, with another person. I have however witnessed a more powerful example of this imaginative play in action, and have seen that when it is distilled, and when it is supported by enough people, we habitually engage with it as reality.

    Additionally, art can now be made in ways with a lower environmental impact than in the past, and digital work could be a way to achieve this as long as we aren't relying on large scale computing.

    Just because everything is derived from nothingness both does and does not mean that our lives (and thus creation) are meaningless. This is another trap for the mind, and part of the reason for this projects' existence is as a means of working out of this trap.

    All creation brings the individual in touch with the vital essence of the universe, and although this is equally achieved through meditation, the focus of section is to explore the possibilities of exploring and transcending the self through work done in the context of sacredness.

    One of the most important things to keep in mind during the process of creation is the very subtle trap of attachment to the thing which is being made. Not only does this get in the way of continued work on a piece, but it reduces the possibilities for what the piece can be through the attachment to what it is. "IF YOU LOVE A SECTION OF A PAINTING, PAINT OVER IT". - Corey Drieth, probably from Picasso.

Her expanse is so truly infinite that materiality spontaneously arises, her face reflected in every facet. She dreampt, and multitudes opened their eyes, believing themselves apart. Those who can allow for spontaneous becoming without attachment are gifted her sweet soma, which washes the dirt from their eyes, revealing in them her infinite compassionate emptiness. She is the primordial artist, who has revealed the way that all has come into being, spontaneously. Her radiance is spontaneous creation, her light is both stone and paint, the world her canvas. It is by following this path that we might understand reality, experiencing the process of becoming through the application of mind or material for the creation of form.

She who is subtle infinity, Clear light cast to form, Her blessings manifest as obliterating embrace In those who fall willing to her arms.

May she shred the ropes we bind with, Freedom as we fall through a void Lit with infinite discovery.

Worlds built reveal paths towards alternate ways of living, breaking through the illusion that we are trapped in only one way of being in the world, providing people with hope and direction towards a more sustainable culture, and the process of creating these worlds will show their creators the path towards understanding the nature of reality. 3.2.1. The System

It is my understanding that the ritualization of actions in service to something percieved to be greater than the individual is useful for the formation of habits which result in continued action, because the perception of sacredness creates an experience of greater importance and deeper meaning.

HOW TO BALANCE THE CRITERIA FOR THE ART WITHOUT ATTACHMENT TO MAKING ART IN THAT WAY

There are a variety of methodologies for the production of artwork, each with varying degrees of reliance on the form constructing imagination present within each human being, and these are generally presented as follows:

It is worth noting that each of these processes have two submethodologies, one of pure discovery, and one of preconception.

  1. A complete lack of visual imagination in the creation of work created directly from life or copied from reference. The external senses are relied upon in near entirety, and this approach will sometimes mix with the next approach to result in the recordings of internal impressions of external experiences, as seen in Mondrian, early Picasso, some academic art, etc.
  2. A near-complete lack of visual imagination in the creation of abstract artwork, with only general impressions of form and colour discovered in its creation.
  3. A relative reliance on the visual imagination for the manipulation of form referenced from either life or media to allow for greater accuracy in the creation of form from imagination. This is very effective for training the individual for the next method.
  4. A complete reliance on the visual imagination, with all form being discovered through the interaction of the individual with the materials which they are utilizing for creation. This can be enacted through inspiration via the creation of shapes, colours, or forms. This category also has varying degrees based on the amount of reliance on memory and previously witnessed form.

Inspiration is both inevitable, and is frequently a mental trap used for justifying a lack of creation. These same categories can be applied to writing, music, math, engineering, and any other undertaking which results in creation. Inspiration can only find you when you're working.

3.3. Rituals

3.3.1. Initiation Ritual

Should you have access to a Resting Place, this can be used, but the important part is that you have somewhere to lay down for a long period of time, in which the entire self can be relaxed in its entirety. This place can be exposed, and it might provide a greater sense of surrender should it be so, but the important part is that it is emotionally comfortable.

Ensure that the shroud is prepared, that the body is wrapped in cloth, marking the body as a possession of the Great Mother.

Take the place where death is to occur, and finish covering yourself with the funerary shroud which has been created. Close your eyes, and begin by taking deep breaths, allowing them to slow down as they will, and begin to feel the embrace of the Great Mother. Understand that this will be the end of who you were, that everything for which you hoped and feared will no longer mean anything, as it will be cast into the void. As who you were had been, so it shall end, as all things will. As you begin to relax, start to visualize the space around you. Visualize wherever it is that you have chosen as your final resting place, and start to step outside of the body, finding your way around the space once you have attained a deep enough absorbtion.

After you are comfortable with the space and the body, invite the Great Mother into the space through whatever means you see fit, and recite the following.

'I give myself over completely to you, Great Mother, who are perfect Emptiness, and pledge to realize your immanence. I give everything I am to you for the benefit of all sentient beings, that this body will become your vessel through which to show the path through attachment and aversion, and which will instantiate your infinite compassion for all sentient beings. Might those who are attached to who I believed to be myself find peace in truth. As the craving for the destruction of the self has been observed, so shall it come to pass, that a lotus might sprout.'

Should the Great Mother of Emptiness choose to inhabit the body, watch as it becomes her vessel, experience it as what was your body becomes a vessel for her infinite compassion, and be grateful that it might be used more effectively as a means of setting sentient beings free from the cycle of suffering. Visualize that she takes your essense, your body, and all that you are, and embraces it, and as she does, see your heart stop beating, your lungs stop breathing, and your blood stop pumping. Allow yourself to dissolve into her arms, letting all experiences melt into her, understanding that as this happens, who you have been is now dead, and there is nothing left of who you were. Witness the body as it begins to decay, watch the crows as they peck through the shroud, returning the body to the earth, and watch as a flock leaves, the Resting Place now empty. Watch as from this emptiness, the body is re-woven from the emptiness, know that it is her will; watch as the flesh begins to take shape, and experience the Great Mother as the body is taken control of, though now interconnected with the entirety of existence.

This is to be used at the onset of work on creative projects.

Visualize the Great Mother before you, and prostrate yourself, saying this prayer to her.

Great Mother, who is all that is, guide this act of creation, that it might become what is needed to eliminate all suffering within the world. All is perfect, yet still suffering exists, so for compassion for all sentient beings realize an act of creation which causes individuals to experience and understand their true nature.

Continue visualizing her as you say this, and offer your entire essence to her in an act of surrender, a reminder that it is she who is this.

Witness her as 'yourself' as she absorbs everything you believed to be yourself, experience the death of the person who you took yourself to be, and feel the change as her infinite compassion enters the body, flowing up the spine and permeating the entirety of the flesh.

Create.

End with the following prayer.

'Through the continued act of creation, all will be nourished with infinite love and compassion, that attachment, aversion, and ignorance will be washed away, and the colourless light of infinite emptiness/everythingness will be realized to permeate completely.'

3.3.3. The Ritual of Shared Worldbuilding

  1. The Ritual Itself

    Begin with two people, dressed in ceremonial robes, or black, whichever is more convenient. They are in a dark room, and they light candles.

    A cleansing ceremony takes place. This begins with rounds of deep breathing as each participant focuses on their breath and slowly finds their way to stillness. After a few minutes of this, a sound is produced.

    'We take our hopes, our fears, our attachments and our aversions, and we lay them before your feet, Oh Great Mother, that we might create without prejudice against the illusions which form our percieved selves.'

    Both parties close their eyes, and sit down.

    At this point, both people visualize a ball of half light and half dark which they visualize sucking all of their hopes, fears, wishes, hatreds, and sadnesses into, and allow it to grow large enough to consume their bodies, sucking out all that which is the suchness of themselves. These spheres are given as an offering to the visualized Mother of Emptiness.

    'Void, your nature, sustainer of life, effective only because of your infinite adaptation, able to fill any form and adapt to any surrounding, providing existence through contrast with matter, infinite in both your stillness and your vibration, revealing form to be at one with you.'

    'Mother void, from whom all of reality is manifest, bless us, that we might bring worlds into being from nothingness. Help us both to empty ourselves that our formless essence might be revealed, that which you are the cause and creator of. We have given you our individual perspectives that we may realize the unity of our essence with all, that we may work as one being to create and experience this universe of our creation, such that we might find the perfect freedom present in spontaneous creation, gaining deeper insight into our true nature in the process.'

    At this point, in the forehead of the Void Mother, an eye opens, endlessly black, and from this eye emanates a complete void which is visualized to fill the entire space, leaving nothing left of the persons which previously stood there.

    'Nothing is now everything, and everything is now nothing. From this place infinite form inevitably manifests. Might all be nourished.'

    Now, the two individuals begin building the world, with either party speaking first when something appears to them, and the process continues until both sides are satisfied with what they have created together. To signal the end of the summoning, one party says 'It is so.'

    'Great Mother Void of whom this is, gratitude exists for the experience of creation. May this permeate all levels of existence, and set all free from the cycles of suffering.'

    The Great Mother is visualized to reveal both identical balls of light and darkness and fuse them, which explodes into being as both participants open their eyes.

    Perhaps at this point tea is made?

    'As this tea is in this water, so are we in the world around us; the world is us and we are the world.'

  2. A Non-ceremonialized version

    I think it is important that I present a version of this experience which isn't within the confines of ritual, because I think that all of the drapery of the previous version might put some people off, and I think that it's important that they experience it regardless.

    Essentially, both people must agree within themselves to be vulnerable with the other, and not to take anything that the other person says too personally. It is easy to be offended by the other person not necessarily building directly off of what you try to add, or disregarding it, and it doesn't mean that it wasn't a good idea, nor does it mean that you aren't good at it. This is the whole reason why I have ritualized this; I want the people experiencing this process together to understand the experience not as themselves, but as mouthpieces for the shared font of creativity, not as individuals which can be hurt by anything the other does.

3.3.4. The Ritual of Inner Journeying

This ritual exists for the purposes of expanding your visualization abilities, and interrogating various aspects of thineself, for the purposes of knowledge and creation. As with all things related to the process of creation, this ought to be done with absolutely no judgement, lest you permanently cut yourself off from the wellspring of creation. This experience is initially intended to be performed alone, and only shared with people with whom you have complete confidance. This is to be practiced every night before finding your way into the gentle embrace of Mother Void, blessed be her name.

Begin by closing your eyes.

Complete seven triangular breathing cycles (7 seconds in, 7 second hold, 7 seconds out).

"Great Mother Emptiness, you of infinite manifestations, who are the womb of creation, I ask thee to share what I need to see to aid in my development and understanding. May you guide me to recognize my true nature, that I might share your gifts, and help to overcome the cycle of suffering."

Begin to notice what you see, whether it be blankness, swirls, colour fields, shapes, etc., and start describing these out loud. Should these begin to develop into more elaborate scenes, describe these, and should they not, describe regardless. Should you find yourself in a place, describe what you see and slowly look around for anything moving. Do not, under any circumstances, try to change anything. Should you find something moving, slowly approach it if you can, and you may find this to be the beginning of a journey. The entities which you encounter may be rather unusual or completely ordinary, but try to avoid being adversarial, for they may be great sources of insight. The most important part of this process is patience. Try to approach everything with a casual curiosity rather than desire, and be content with anything which you experience.

When performing this ritual before sleep, you can forgo the closing until the morning.

"You of infinite manifestations, whose creations ring with the endless love of sponteneity, thank you for guiding me in this experience. Might I continue to be your faithful student, such that I might realize what I am, and use this knowledge for the benefit of all that exists."

You may find that you want to record your experiences as you are having them, which is fine, just be sure to treat them as the sacred gifts which they are. When recording (whether by audio or through writing), be sure to avoid trying to edit until after your experience has been had.
